## What Changed This Week?

This week wasn't really about training.

It was more about being prepared.

I ended up going out for the World Cup games twice, and I didn't really plan ahead with food.

There weren't many options that you'd consider bodybuilding prep friendly.

I could have skipped eating, but I knew that wasn't the right choice either.

One day I ended up getting a cheesesteak.

The other day I had sushi.

Neither one was ideal, but they were both high in protein.

I made my best guess on the macros, logged everything, and then just kept eating according to plan for the rest of the day.

The morning after both meals my weight was up a little.

Honestly, I expected that.

It was probably just the extra sodium more than anything.

By the end of the week I was back to hitting every meal exactly like I wanted to, and my weight ended up lower than it had been all prep.

Looking back, I don't really regret what I ate.

I just should have planned ahead better.

That was honestly what I was hoping for.

The extra cardio seems like it was enough, so I'd rather leave everything alone than start making more changes just because I can.

The visual analysis still has me a little behind.

This week it called out my abs and lower back.

I already had a feeling my abs were lagging, so that wasn't much of a surprise.

The lower back honestly caught me off guard. It wasn't something I'd really been paying attention to until this week's check-in.

## My Thoughts

The abs are probably the only thing I'm thinking about right now.

I'll admit they worry me a little.

It makes me wonder if I gave myself enough time for this prep.

I probably could have started a little earlier.

At the same time, I don't think it's time to panic.

The weight is moving again.

I'm making progress.

There are still a lot of weeks left.

If things slow down again, I'll probably have to get more aggressive.

For now though, I'm just going to trust the process and keep doing what I'm doing.
