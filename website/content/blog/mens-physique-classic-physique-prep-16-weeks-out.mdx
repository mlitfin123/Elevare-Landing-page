# Prep Files #03

Another week down.

This week was interesting because it reminded me that progress and perfection are not the same thing.

If you asked me how closely I followed the plan, I'd tell you it wasn't great.

If you asked me whether progress happened anyway, I'd tell you yes.

That's one of the reasons prep can be mentally challenging. It's easy to focus on everything you're not doing perfectly and completely miss the fact that you're still moving in the right direction.

For now, I'll take the win.

## What Changed This Week?

Honestly, the biggest thing I noticed this week is that I'm still not fully locked in.

If I had to guess, I'd say I was around 60–65% compliant this week.

Not terrible, but definitely not where I need to be.

The good news is that even with that, things seem to be moving in the right direction. My weight is down, my abs feel a little tighter, and overall I look a little better than I did a week ago.

Whether that's actual fat loss or just the result of cleaning up my food and adding cardio, I'm not really sure yet.

Either way, I'll take it.

More than anything, I feel like I'm starting to get back into prep mode mentally.

The first couple weeks are always the weirdest part for me. You're not fully committed yet, but you're also not in the offseason anymore.

This week felt like the transition between those two.

## My Thoughts

I think this week was probably more important mentally than physically.

There's always a little anxiety at the start of prep.

You look at the timeline, look at where you're starting, and start doing the math in your head.

Do I have enough time?

Am I lean enough?

Should I be pushing harder already?

I've been through enough preps to know those thoughts are normal.

Right now I'm trying not to get ahead of myself.

The goal isn't to be stage ready today.

The goal is to be a little better next week than I am right now.

So far, I think that's happening.
